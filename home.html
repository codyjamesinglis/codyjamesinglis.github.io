I’m a historian of political thought, focusing on East Central Europe during the ‘long’ nineteenth and ‘short’ twentieth centuries. I completed my PhD in Comparative History at Central European University in Vienna, Austria (previously in Budapest, Hungary), and my MA in the same field at the same institution.

Currently, I work on the history of democratic political thought and conceptual history, but I also have interests in the philosophy of history, the history of philosophy, the history of historiography, and issues related to the historical sociology of knowledge.

I’m based at the CEU Democracy Institute in Budapest, where I work as part of the Democracy in History Work Group.
