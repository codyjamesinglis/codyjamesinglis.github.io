I am an intellectual historian of East Central Europe during the nineteenth and twentieth centuries, focusing spatially on the Habsburg Empire and its successor states. While my published work to date lies mainly in the history of political thought, I have broader research interests in conceptual history, the history of philosophy, the history of historiography, the philosophy of history, and issues related to the sociology and epistemology of historical consciousness (elsewhere better known as Geschichtsbewußtsein).

I completed my PhD in Comparative History at Central European University in Vienna, Austria (previously in Budapest, Hungary). My dissertation, entitled “Between Freedom and Constraint: Republicanism and the Left in Hungary and Yugoslavia, 1918–1948,” can be found here.

I’m currently based at the CEU Democracy Institute (DI) in Budapest, where I work as a Research Affiliate in the Democracy in History Work Group. As part of our activities, I run a postdoctoral project called “Clio in a Phrygian Cap: Historicist Epistemology and Democratic Political Thought in Twentieth-Century East Central Europe.”
